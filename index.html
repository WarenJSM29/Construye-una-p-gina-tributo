Nikola Tesla

THE GENIUS WHO LIT THE WORLD

Here's a time line of Mr. Tesla's life:

Studies

  • 1856 - Nikola Tesla was born on July 10, 1856 in Smiljan, Croatia.
  • 1861 - He attended elementary school in Smiljan, where he studied arithmetic and religion.
  • 1862 - The family moved to Gospic, Austrian Empire, where his father worked as a shepherd.
  • 1870 - He moved to Karlovac, Croatia, to attend the Gospic Royal Gymnasium, where he was influenced by his mathematics teacher Martin Sekulic.
  • 1873 - Graduating in 1873, he returned to his hometown where shortly after his arrival he contracted cholera. He was bedridden for nine months and near death on several occasions. His father promised to send him to the best engineering school if he recovered from the illness (his father wanted him to become a priest).
  • 1874 - Nikola Tesla avoided his recruitment by the Austro-Hungarian army by fleeing to Tomingaj, near Gracac.
  • 1875 - During 1875, he enrolled thanks to a scholarship at the Polytechnic School of Graz, Austria.
  • 1878 - He never graduated from university and in December 1878, he left Graz and cut ties with his family trying to hide his dropout from them.
  • 1879 - On March 24, 1879, he was returned to Gospic under police surveillance for not having a residence permit. On April 17, 1879, his mother died, and during that year, he taught students at his old school.
  • 1880 - In January 1880, two of his uncles raise enough money to send him to Prague where he intended to study at the university, but unfortunately, he arrived too late to enroll; He also did not study Greek or Czech, compulsory subjects. Nikola Tesla attended lectures at the university, although, as an auditor, he never received course grades.

Engineer

  • 1881 - He moved to Budapest to work for Ferenc Puskas where he became head electrician and later engineer for the country's first telephone system. During his employment, he made many improvements to central station equipment and claimed to have perfected a telephone that was never patented.

Edison Company

  • 1882 - He joined the Continental Edison Company in France, to design and improve electrical equipment.
  • 1884 - In June 1884, he moved to New York City where he was hired by Thomas Edison to work at the Edison Machine Works designing motors and generators, but left to devote himself exclusively to experimental research and invention.
  • 1885 - In 1885, he argued that he could redesign Edison's motors and generators to improve their serviceability and economy. According to Tesla, Edison told him, "I have fifty thousand dollars for you if you can do it." After months of work he completed the task and demanded payment. Edison told him that he was joking, and he replied: "You don't understand our American humor." In return, he offered her a raise from $10 a week at 18. Tesla rejected the offer and fired.

Tesla Electric Light & Manufacturing

  • 1886 - He founded his own company, Tesla Electric Light & Manufacturing. Investors disagreed with his plans for the development of an alternating current motor, relieving him of his position at the company.

Alternating current

  • 1888 - During 1888, his first practical system design for generating and transmitting alternating current for electrical power systems took place...
  • ...Nikola Tesla's alternating current (AC) is a type of electrical current in which the direction of the current is periodically reversed. This means that current alternates between flowing in one direction and then flowing in another. AC is the most common type of electrical current used in the world today...
  • ...He developed the alternating current induction motor, eliminating the commutator and ignition brushes of direct current motors and introduced improvements in the field of transmission and generation of alternating current energy, noting that both the generation and transmission of this they could be obtained much more efficiently with an alternating current than in the case of direct current, the most commonly used at that time...
  • ...The rights to this invention, momentous at the time, were bought by the American inventor George Westinghouse, who showed the system for the first time at the World's Columbian Exposition in Chicago (1893). Two years later Tesla's AC motors were installed in the Niagara Falls Electric Power Design.

Wardencliff Tower

  • 1893 - Nikola Tesla designed a wireless communication system and built an antenna over 30 meters high, the Wardencliff Tower, with which he intended to transmit electrical energy wirelessly, with the same operating principle as radio.

Inventions

  • 1893 - Before the Wright brothers' maiden flight, he tested a prototype of the first vertical takeoff aircraft.
  • 1894/1895 - A year later, experimenting with discharge tubes, he realized that "invisible radiation" appeared, later called X-rays, which damaged photographic film stored nearby. He continued to investigate this line, but all his work was lost in a fire in his laboratory in March 1895.
  • 1898 - He patented more than 700 inventions such as an electric submarine in 1898 and a small ship that would capture energy emitted by the Wardencliff Tower that would be stored in its batteries...
  • ...In the opinion of some Serbian scientists, the Sojourner's operating system is based on the patent for the electric submarine designed by Tesla.
  • In 1898 he developed and registered with the United States Patent and Trademark Office the first remote control electrical device. He presented it in December of that same year at an electricity fair. With which he managed a small boat wirelessly, to the astonishment of the public. The device would be the forerunner of drones and the remote control of the TV.

Death

  • 1943 - Nikola Tesla died of an acute myocardial infarction on January 7, 1943, in room 3327 of the New Yorker Hotel, New York City (United States). His body was found after ignoring the "do not disturb" sign he had posted on the door two days earlier.
